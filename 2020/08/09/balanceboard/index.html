How i made a balance board for 5€

Balance boards seem to have gotten The Next Cool Thing (according to personalized advertising on various platforms, at least). And since those algorithms know me so well, i immediately wanted one. So, i did the only logical thing: built my own instead of spending a smaaaaall amount of money (100€ upwards) on a board out of a shop like Costa Board or Vew Do (although they do look really cool and i admire Vew Do’s roller system. Check them out, if you want to).

The result cost me only a small fraction of that and 2 hours of my time. Worth it.

MATERIALS

  • Wooden board (mine was 80 x 40 x 1,8 cm, doesn’t matter what sort of wood)
  • Wooden fence post (diameter ~ 8cm, 25cm long)
  • Some felt for wrapping the fence post
  • Saw (sawing by hand saves you an arm day at the gym)
  • Sanding paper
  • Glue
  • Colour and/or wood finish, if you feel fancy

These materials (saw, sanding paper and glue not included) cost me 5,33€ at my local crafting store.

HOW TO

Get your wood board and draw on the shape that you want. You’ll find inspiration easily online, e.g. at the shops mentioned above or from looking up surf- or longboards.

Now, saw the board along your lines and sand the edges. I wanted mine to be rather smooth with reeeally round edges, but I just had to stop after an hour. So, semi-round it is for me.

Saw the edges of your fence post (and shorten it, if it is too long – it should be no longer than your board width if you plan on surfing horizontally. But you can make different rollers with various diameters and lengths!)

Glue the felt onto the roller. Some tutorials i have seen recommend cork, a carpet patch or some kind of thin foam instead of felt. Take whatever you have available and what feels best. But remember to let it dry completely.

Technically, you’re ready to go now. I strongly recommend applying some kind of varnish or wood oil to the board, as it shows off the wood texture. You can also paint your board and/or roller now to give it a personal touch.

TIPS

The making of the board is kind of self-explanatory, if you have the tools needed (or know someone you can borrow them from) and know how to handle them. But there are certain things i realized during the process, that might come in handy if you try to build your own:

Many tutorials recommend using a pvc pipe as a roller. I totally see where this is coming from, a lookthrough roller seems pretty cool. If you decide to use one, make sure it supports your weight! I, a girl of 70kg, did not trust it, but the decision is up to you.

When looking for a roller, keep different diameters in mind. Different diameters change how much you will sway from side to side when surfing and how high above the ground you will float.

Keep in mind, whether you’ll use your board more outdoors or indoors. Save your floors, glue some felt or foam to the board tips if you have something like hardwood floor. If your room has a carpet floor, even bare wood for the roller should be fine.
